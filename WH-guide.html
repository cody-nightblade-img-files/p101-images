Introduction

Recently, I've been wondering if there were any known specific details about Witch Hunter.
Search results weren't helpful, so I decided to investigate with the help of one of my friends, ultzx.
After about one month, we figured out how exactly it works.

This information can be helpful to others, so I have created this guide.
I will discuss how Witch Hunter works, including examples to help with understanding.

What is Witch Hunter?

In case you are not familiar, Witch Hunter allows the user to make a bonus attack if the enemy uses magic on them.
This attack occurs before the magic attack that activated it.
It also activates before Intuition.

It also states that it reduces damage taken by 50% for the incoming magical attack that activated it.
(The in-game text states "-50% Weapon Power vs Magic." It does not actually reduce Weapon Power, just damage taken.)

General details by rank:
1 May activate once per turn.
2 May activate up to 3 times per turn.
3 If the bonus attack hits, boosts the user's Resist for 3 turns.
4 The bonus attack has +5% Critical Chance.
5 If the bonus attack hits, it may purge buffs from the enemy.

How do you train Witch Hunter?

Certain companions can train Witch Hunter.
(most Witchdoctor companions + some Privateer Companions, like Wing Chun and Lucky Jack Russel)
Pirates can obtain up to 3 ranks of Witch Hunter.

Ways to get Witch Hunter ranks:

  • trained from Kurotadori (the Witchdoctor Clandestine Trainer) for 1 Practice Point.
    (required: Level 20+, Staffy Weapons 2)
  • granted by Phule's Wand (dropped in the Bruno Chapel instance)
  • granted by a pet with the Grants Witch Hunter pet talent

What kind of attack does Witch Hunter deal?

The bonus attack from Witch Hunter actually depends on the user's basic attack type -- melee, ranged, or magic.

If the user with Witch Hunter is a....

  • melee unit -- Witch Hunter deals a melee attack
  • ranged unit -- Witch Hunter deals a ranged attack
  • magic unit -- Witch Hunter deals a magic attack
    (If the enemy has Witch Hunter, then this may cause their Witch Hunter to activate, causing a Witch Hunter chain.)

How exactly does Witch Hunter activate?

The game states that Witch Hunter activates against incoming enemy magic.
However, the bonus attack and damage reduction only occur under certain conditions, meaning that it's possible for Witch Hunter to do absolutely nothing when attacked with magic.

Witch Hunter's Damage Reduction

Witch Hunter's damage reduction requires being next to the unit attacking you with magic.
In this regard, it favors melee units, since they will naturally be adjacent to their attackers.
Ranged and magic units can still benefit, but they're much less likely to be next to enemy magic users.

Witch Hunter's Bonus Attack

For the bonus attack to occur, one of the following conditions must be met:

  • You must be affected by the magic attack.
  • You must have the same attacking type (melee / ranged / magic) as the unit directly targeted by magic.
    (This is always true if you are the directly-selected target.)

Do note both effects may only happen up to 3 times / turn (once per turn if rank 1).

My pirate is casting Mojo Storm, directly targeting Old Scratch to hit two Bone Drakes, both of which have Witch Hunter 1.

Since Old Scratch attacks with magic, only magic-attacking enemies could counter with Witch Hunter.
Thus, the Bone Drake next to my Pirate did not use Witch Hunter (it's a melee attacker).
However, since it was next to my Pirate, it only took 50% damage.

An interesting case with Witch Hunter.....

Something to note about how this Epic activates is that targeting out of range still uses one of the Epic's bonus attack(s).
This wastes 1 fire from the user with Witch Hunter (even though the bonus attack wasn't used).

Its stated base damage was 18, though, meaning that the attack was halved.

Because of these interactions, it's easier to state the bonus attack and damage reduction act independently of each other.

How much Resistance does Witch Hunter 3+ give?

As I've stated in a different thread, the Resistance you gain from Witch Hunter 3:

  • is equal to 25% of your total Spell Power, rounded down
    (Scratch greatly helps here with his Spell Power buffs!)
  • lasts for 3 turns
  • does not show up as a buff during turn selection

You can only gain Resistance from Witch Hunter 3+ when you successfully hit the enemy with it. Since it lasts for 3 turns, you can stack them to insane values, such as:

How's Witch Hunter's base Damage determined?

Here's Witch Hunter's base Damage formula:

base = 1.35 * [Spell Power] * (1 + 0.002 * (main stat))

As you can see, it uses Spell Power and the user's main stat (Strength / Agility / Will).
Like other Epics, it may deal Physical or Magical Damage, depending on the weapon.

Summary

  • Witch Hunter activates against incoming magic attacks.
  • Depending on the user, Witch Hunter may deal a melee, ranged, or magic attack to the enemy.
  • You can have up to 3 ranks of Witch Hunter via training it or using items that grant it (Phule's Wand + pet).
  • Witch Hunterâ€™s damage reduction and bonus attack work separately from each other.
  • The damage reduction only works when adjacent to the enemy attacker.
    It works independent of the bonus attack the Epic grants.
  • The bonus attack only occurs if you are affected and have the same attack type (melee / ranged / magic) as the directly-targeted unit (which can be yourself).
  • Being affected by enemy magic will use the bonus attack's fires, regardless of whether the above condition is met.
  • Resistance gained from Witch Hunter is based on the user's total Spell Power and lasts for 3 turns.
  • Witch Hunter's base damage is based on the user's total Spell Power and main stat.
